~ SARON ~

Origin

The saron is a traditional Indonesian percussion instrument, originating from Java and Bali.

Technique

Played by striking bronze keys with a mallet while damping previous notes with the other hand to produce clear, precise melodies.

Symbolism

The melodic backbone of gamelan music, representing harmony and the structure of the composition.

Function

Its main role is to carry the core melody, known as the balungan, providing a foundation for other instruments to elaborate upon.

Ensemble

Part of the gamelan ensemble, working alongside gongs, kendang, and other metallophones to create layered textures.

Material

Made of bronze bars placed over a wooden resonating frame (rancakan).
