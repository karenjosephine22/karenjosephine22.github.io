~ GONG ~

Origin

The gong is one of Indonesia’s oldest musical instruments, used across many islands such as Java, Bali, and Sulawesi.

Technique

Played by striking the center boss with a padded mallet, creating deep resonant tones.

Symbolism

Represents balance, harmony, and spiritual energy in Indonesian culture.

Function

Often used in traditional gamelan ensembles to mark time and transitions in musical compositions.

Ensemble

Central to gamelan orchestras, balancing rhythm and melody within the group.

Material

Made from bronze or brass and carefully tuned to a specific pitch.
