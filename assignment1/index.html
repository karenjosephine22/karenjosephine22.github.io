1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?
The use of colour was the first thing that immediately caught my eye. The bright, neon tones created a strong visual impact and set the mood for the experience from the get-go. The palette was both playful and energetic, and it kind of suggests that the website was more of an artwork or interactive website rather than a traditional portfolio. It creates a good first impression where it communicates personality and creative intent, before I even begin reading the text. It also hints at the type of designer behind the work; someone who values bold expression and wants their site to feel immersive rather than purely informational.

When I first arrived on the website, I moved my mouse around the landing section and quickly noticed that certain words changed when hovering over I skimmed through the introduction text while experimenting with the cursor movements. 3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?
I actually spent the most time on the first section of the website, the introduction or "about me" page. This was mainly because of the interactive words that changed when I hovered my cursor over them. I found myself experimenting repeatedly, moving the mouse across the text to see what new words would appear. This shows how even a small but playful feature can capture attention more effectively than larger visuals. It invites the user to participate actively rather than passively scroll.
4. What was the most common action in your two-minute interaction with the experience?
Moving my cursor around the screen was the most common action I did. Unlike traditional websites where scrolling or clicking dominates, the interaction here felt centred on hovering and exploring how the text or visuals responded to my movements. I found myself less focused on reading every single line of text and more on experimenting with where the cursor could uncover hidden responses. I think the designer intentionally made hovering the primary form of engagement, encouraging playful exploration.
5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?
I’d say that the primary goal of this interactive experience is to show the designer’s creativity, technical skills, and their passion for building interactive websites. Rather than serving as a straightforward portfolio with project links and case studies, the site itself becomes the portfolio. It shows what the designer is capable of: immersing visitors in an interactive environment where the emphasis seems less on providing detailed information and more on leaving a strong impression of originality.
6) How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?
The experience communicates its goal effectively through the use of consistent interactivity, bold visuals, and experimental effects. Each section includes a different form of interaction, from hover text to moving shapes and pixel effects. The strong colour palette and unconventional structure also communicate that this is not a typical website, but rather a showcase of creative identity. Palat2023 demonstrates their ability to think beyond standard layouts and tools, aligning the medium with the message (making the site itself into a piece of digital art).
